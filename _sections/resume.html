I currently work as a research programmer for the Department of Atmospheric Sciences at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign (U of I), as well as regular appearances at the National Center for Supercomputing Applications. I graduated from the U of I in 2018 with a Bachelor's in Computer Engineering. My specializations primarily revolve around high performance computing, including parallel programming (CUDA, MPI, OpenMP), system administration, big data management, and software development.

One of the largest motivations for me to study computers is to advanced the scientific pursuit. I take great pride and satisfaction in work that helps to further scientific research. I enjoy fields such as quantum physics, cosmology, astrophysics, climatology, and biology.
